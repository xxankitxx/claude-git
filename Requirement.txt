## 4. Open Interest & Support/Resistance Engine (The Core Differentiator)
This is the heart of the system. **It does not use price‑based S/R alone**, but combines OI concentration, OI change acceleration, PCR, and order flow.

### 4.1. OI Zone Construction
For each underlying (index and stock), the system:
- **Tracks OI by strike** for both CE and PE.
- **Identifies OI walls:** Strikes where OI > 2× average of neighbouring strikes (configurable).
- **Calculates OI change rate:** d(OI)/dt using exponential moving average over 1, 3, 5 minutes.
- **Computes PCR per strike and aggregate PCR (all strikes).**
- **Definition per your specification:**  
  - **Put OI created by bulls** (hedging/protection). **Call OI created by bears** (covered calls or speculative).  
  - High PCR at a strike → that strike is being defended by bulls → strong support.  
  - Low PCR (call heavy) → resistance.
- **Therefore:**
  - **Support zone** = cluster of strikes where PCR > 1 and Put OI is increasing with positive acceleration.
  - **Resistance zone** = cluster where PCR < 0.7 and Call OI is increasing with negative acceleration.
- **Dynamic tracking:** The system re‑computes these zones every time OI changes; they shift intraday.

### 4.2. Combined S/R from Multiple Heavyweights
Since Nifty/Bank Nifty/Fin Nifty are weighted averages:
- For each index constituent, compute individual S/R zones from its own options OI.
- Map constituent S/R levels back to index points using the constituent’s beta and weight.
- The final index S/R is a weighted blend of the index’s own option OI zones **and** the implied zones from top constituents.
- Example: If Reliance OI suggests strong support at ₹2,500, and Reliance has 9% weight in Nifty, the system translates that to a Nifty support contribution = 2500 * 0.09 / Reliance’s correlation factor.

### 4.3. Additional OI‑Based Signals
- **Max Pain:** Calculate the strike where total option buyer pain (CE+PE) is minimized; often acts as a magnetic level.
- **OI change divergence:** If price rises but call OI falls (calls being covered by bears) → bearishness fading → potential long.
- **Volume‑weighted OI delta:** For each strike, compute the net delta of all options (using Black‑Scholes delta) weighted by OI; the cumulative delta gives a market‑maker hedging flow indication.

## 5. Greeks & Volatility Models — Where & How They Are Used
You asked the AI to decide. Here’s the intelligent design:

### 5.1. Real‑Time Greek Calculation
- **Black‑Scholes‑Merton:** Used for all option contracts (European assumption works well for indices). Compute Delta, Gamma, Theta, Vega, Rho, Charm, Vanna, Vomma every 200 ms using compiled `py_vollib` or a Numba‑accelerated custom function.
- **Greeks are used in trade decisions:**
  - **Delta** to gauge directional exposure; option delta of 0.4 preferred for intraday moves.
  - **Gamma** to assess stability; avoid high gamma near expiry unless breakout expected.
  - **Theta** to consider time decay cost; only enter positions when expected move (from expected move formula) exceeds theta decay over planned holding period.
  - **Vega** to capture IV expansion opportunities (e.g., pre‑event).
  - **Charm/Vanna** to predict delta changes with time/vol; advanced adjustment for trade management.

### 5.2. Advanced Models (Heston, SABR, GARCH)
- **Purpose:** Validate support/resistance and gauge market regime, not for real‑time pricing.
- **Heston model** calibrated every 5 minutes to produce forward volatility smile; if OTM puts are overpriced relative to Heston fair value, it suggests strong hedging demand (bullish).
- **SABR** for fine‑tuning skew interpolation.
- **GARCH(1,1) / GJR‑GARCH** on spot returns to forecast short‑term volatility regime. High forecasted vol → prefer debit spreads or limited‑risk strategies; low vol → outright options.
- **IV Rank & IV Percentile** (daily, weekly, monthly) to determine if premiums are historically cheap or expensive — influences stop‑loss and target scaling (wider targets in high IV environments).

### 5.3. Volatility Surface & Skew
- Live 3D volatility surface (moneyness vs. expiry) is a ready‑reckoner panel.
- **Skew index:** (IV of 25‑delta put – IV of 25‑delta call). Steepening skew → rising fear → bullish for puts or bearish? The system will interpret based on context (if skew steepens while price holds, bullish; if price drops, bearish continuation).

## 7. Trade Decision Engine (Pure Rules, No ML Unless Supplementary)
The engine operates in three steps:

### 7.1. Pre‑Filter (Market Structure)
- **Time filter:** No trades in first 5 minutes of market open; no new positions after 14:30.
- **Major events:** Check economic calendar (via API) — avoid 30 minutes around major news.
- **Volatility filter:** If IV Rank < 20 and ADX(14) < 20 → no trade (chop).

### 7.2. Signal Generation
The system will evaluate long CE and long PE separately. Only the direction with a signal score above a threshold (0.7) will be considered.

**Long CE Signal Score = weighted sum of:**
1. **Futures CVD trend:** +0.25 if CVD rising and price above VWAP.
2. **Composite S/R:** +0.25 if price bounced off a strong OI support zone (from index + constituents).
3. **PCR flip:** +0.15 if aggregate PCR crossed above 1.2 after being below.
4. **OI divergence:** +0.1 if call OI is dropping while price rises.
5. **IV Rank:** +0.1 if IV Rank > 30 (cheap premium) *or* IV expanding.
6. **Momentum:** +0.1 if SuperTrend (10,3) flipped bullish and RSI(14) > 50.
7. **Volume confirmation:** +0.05 if CE bid/ask volume imbalance > 1.5 (buyers dominating).
8. **Greeks alignment:** +0.05 if at‑the‑money option Delta > 0.4 and Gamma < 0.01 (stable).
   *Total possible = 1.05; threshold = 0.70.*

**Long PE Signal Score (mirror, with opposite criteria).**

### 7.3. Trade Execution & Management
- **Capital utilisation:** `lots = floor(100000 / (premium * lot_size))`. Premium is LTP of selected option.
- **Strike selection:** The system picks the strike that:
  - Is closest to the expected move (Expected Move = 0.8 * ATM straddle price) *plus* 10 points.
  - Has highest liquidity (tight bid‑ask spread).
  - Is just outside the nearest OI barrier (to avoid strike where time decay is extreme if price stalls).
- **Entry:** Immediately upon signal threshold breach, place a simulated market order at LTP.
- **Stop‑Loss:** Set at entry premium – 10 points. Hard exit if hit.
- **Target & Dynamic Trailing:**
  - Initial target = entry premium + 12 points.
  - Once premium rises by 6 points, the system activates **dynamic trailing**:
    - Trailing stop starts at entry premium + 1 point (so a 1‑point giveback triggers exit if price reverses after 6‑point gain).
    - As profit increases, the trailing stop advances based on a **volatility‑adjusted parameter**: trailed stop = max(previous stop, current price – (ATR(14) of option premium)).
    - This captures larger moves while locking in 6+ points minimum.
- **Trade strength monitoring:** After entry, the system continuously re‑evaluates the signal score. If the score drops below 0.4, it triggers a “weakening” alert and tightens the trailing stop to exit at next pullback, even if not at target/SL.
